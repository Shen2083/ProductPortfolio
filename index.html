Philosophy: Behavioural Science in Product

My approach goes beyond feature delivery; I leverage behavioural economics to align product architecture with human decision-making. By reducing cognitive load and utilizing "nudge" theory, I drive adoption that is natural and durable.

Nudge Theory

Structuring intuitive decision pathways that guide users toward high-value actions (e.g., Job Optimisation) without restricting freedom, moving from friction to flow.

Cognitive Fluency

Simplifying complex data (Campaigns 2.0) to reduce the mental effort required to make decisions, directly impacting conversion and retention rates.

Push-Pull Alignment

Synchronizing the user's intrinsic motivation ("Push") with the product's extrinsic value proposition ("Pull") to ensure sustainable growth.

Campaigns 2.0 & Talent Scout AI

Core Innovation

Problem & Context

Legacy "Objective Campaigns" imposed high cognitive load on employers, requiring intricate manual configuration. This complexity acted as a barrier to effective budget utilization.

Innovation & Role

As DRI, I led the reimagining of the sponsorship framework. We applied behavioural simplification, moving to a "Performance-Driven" model with job-level budget controls. This backend logic laid the foundation for Talent Scout, Indeed's first external-facing AI agent.

Impact

  • 25% revenue uplift in pilots.
  • 59% increase in jobs receiving applications.
  • Reduction in user error rates due to simplified architecture.
